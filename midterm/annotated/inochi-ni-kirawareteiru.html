This part reflects on the speaker's motivations and ego. They acknowledge that their reluctance to face the death of a loved one stems from their own discomfort or inability to cope with the emotional pain it would bring. The phrase "because I won't like it" suggests that their ego is unable to handle the emotional distress associated with loss, highlighting a vulnerability or weakness within themselves.
